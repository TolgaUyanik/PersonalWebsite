Stop Chasing Two Rabbits: The Simple Truth Behind Extraordinary Results

How to Find Your ONE Thing and Unleash the Domino Effect

"If you chase two rabbits, you will not catch either one."
— Russian Proverb

We've all heard the Russian proverb: "If you chase two rabbits, you will not catch either one". Yet, in our professional lives, we constantly try to chase ten. We manage massive to-do lists, pride ourselves on multitasking, and treat every item on our list as if it matters equally.

The groundbreaking book, The ONE Thing: The Surprisingly Simple Truth Behind Extraordinary Results, debunks this chaotic approach. Its central premise is simple: to achieve extraordinary results, you must Go Small. This means ignoring all the things you could do and focusing intensely on the ONE Thing you should do.

1. The Core Idea: Success is Built Sequentially

The book challenges the prevailing myths of achievement, identifying "The Six Lies Between You and Success":

  • Everything Matters Equally
  • Multitasking
  • A Disciplined Life
  • Willpower Is Always on Will-Call
  • A Balanced Life
  • Big Is Bad

The authors emphasize that the biggest hurdle is believing that everything matters equally. It simply isn't true. This is where the Pareto Principle (or 80/20 Rule) comes in. The 80/20 Principle asserts that a minority of your causes, inputs, or effort usually leads to a majority of the results, outputs, or rewards. A small amount of causes creates most of the results.

The ONE Thing is the ultimate application of this principle—it's focusing on the vital few (the 20%) that produce the maximal results (the 80%).

2. The Power of the Focusing Question

If you're only supposed to do one thing, how do you find it? You use the Focusing Question. This single, powerful question is the ultimate tool for cutting through clutter and identifying your most leveraged activity:

"What's the ONE Thing I can do [in this area] such that by doing it everything else will be easier or unnecessary?"

This question has three parts, all designed to find your most important action:

  • PART 1: The ONE Thing: It forces you to narrow your concentration and find a singular activity.
  • PART 2: Such that by doing it...: This is the criterion your answer must meet, creating a bridge between simply doing something and doing something for a specific, leveraged purpose.
  • PART 3: ...Everything else will be easier or unnecessary?: This is the ultimate leverage test. It tells you when you've found the first domino. When you accomplish this task, other things you thought you had to do will require less effort or may no longer be necessary at all.

You can apply this question to any area of your life: your job, your finances, your health, or your key relationships.

3. The Domino Effect and Time Blocking

The reason this focused approach works is the Domino Effect: Success is built sequentially, one thing at a time.

Imagine a set of dominoes where each one is 50 percent larger than the one preceding it. The first tiny domino can, in a geometric progression, ultimately knock over a domino almost the distance to the moon. In life, when the right thing is set in motion, it can topple many successively larger things.

To harness this power, the book lays out a simple formula for extraordinary results: Purpose, Priority, and Productivity.

  1. Purpose: This is your long-term direction, or your "big ONE Thing".
  2. Priority: This is the action you take to move toward your purpose, your "small ONE Thing".
  3. Productivity: This is achieved through Time Blocking.

Time Blocking Your ONE Thing:

If disproportionate results come from one activity, you must give that one activity disproportionate time. Successful people don't just put their ONE Thing on a to-do list; they put it on their calendar and protect that time fiercely.

Your daily priority is to ask: "Today, what's the ONE Thing I can do for my ONE Thing such that by doing it everything else will be easier or unnecessary?". Block time for that answer, and you will be performing the most leveraged activity for your work, ensuring results become extraordinary.

What is Your First Domino?

The journey to extraordinary results doesn't start with a giant leap—it starts with a single, focused step. It starts with the answer to your Focusing Question.

What is the ONE Thing you can do right now that will make everything else in your current project or goal easier or unnecessary? Find that first domino, knock it over, and watch the chain reaction begin.
