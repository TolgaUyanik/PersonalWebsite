Lesson 1: What I Wish Someone Told Me When I Was Broke

When Alex started his first brick-and-mortar gym at 23, he didn't know you could hire employees. He cleaned, did billing, sales, taught sessions, and fixed equipment himself. Every night, he would think: "If I didn't wake up tomorrow, I would be okay with it."

The Foundation: Two Words

"My fault." The first lesson of getting out of poverty. Until you own everything that led to this moment, you cannot change it. Whatever you cast blame to is where you cast power to.

The Glass Metaphor

Pulling the Rope of Life

Imagine your life's timeline as a rope you're pulling toward yourself. But the rope has different textures:

  1. Glass (beginning) — Cuts your hands, extremely painful
  2. Coarse/Rough — Still hurts, but less bloody
  3. Neutral — Tolerable
  4. Sweet — Life starts rewarding you

When you work harder than normal (doing your job PLUS building something on the side), you're pulling more rope per day. You're living "three days at a time" — getting through the glass section faster.

"On a long enough time horizon, everyone would eventually be successful, but people wait too long and they die too early."

The Trap of Delayed Pain

Poor People Successful People
Delay pain ("I'll start Monday") Take pain now for pleasure later
Take pleasure now, pain later Delay gratification
Surprised when the bill comes due Front-load the cost

Showing Up On Time

People watch YouTube for complex money-making skills but haven't mastered showing up on time. They want calculus when they can't do addition.

"The basics don't make them less important. They're the foundation of a building — arguably more important. They're just simpler to understand but not simpler to do."

The disconnect: people think that because they understand something conceptually, they've mastered the skill. Understanding discipline ≠ being disciplined.

Lesson 2: How To Win In Your 20s

Alex advocates for a 6-day, 12-hour work week for men aged 16-28. "You are valued based on your utility. When you're young, you have none. But you have energy. Trade that energy for experience points."

The "Very Broke" Five-Point Plan

If you have less than $100,000 in savings:

  1. Stop eating out. Discount groceries only.
  2. Stop buying new clothes. Goodwill only.
  3. Only attend FREE friend events. If they don't support your goals, they're not your friends — they're your enemies.
  4. Use spare time to: Apply to better-paying jobs, pick up side hustles, learn higher-paying skills.
  5. Find the hardest worker and DOUBLE their output. If they knock 100 doors, you knock 200.
  6. Wait 36 months.

Patient with Outputs, Impatient with Inputs

You can't control when money comes (output). You CAN control what you do (input). When feeling impatient about results, use action as your coping mechanism.

The Tree Metaphor

"You can poison a tree in a second. You just can't grow one real fast. But it doesn't mean that if a seed's planted and you're watering it, it's not growing."

High-Volume Sales: The Common Thread

Every successful friend Alex knows started in high-volume sales environments: door-knocking, cold calling, or in-person businesses with many daily conversations with strangers. Not for a month or six months — for 3-5 years.

Skill development = Volume × Feedback Loops

The Banana Story

A foreign kid who had never seen a banana bit right into it with the skin. Another child called him an idiot. The adult said: "No, he just doesn't know any better."

There's a big difference between being dumb and being inexperienced. You're not stupid — you just don't have reps yet.

Lesson 3: Building Your Confidence

The Core Truth

"Don't build confidence. Build evidence."

Confidence is simply a prediction — the likelihood that what you think will happen, will happen. How do you increase that? Have it have happened already.

Confidence Without Evidence = Delusion

Wrong Approach Right Approach
30 min daily mirror pep talks Review the script
"Fake it till you make it" "Walk it before you talk it"
Confidence without evidence Certainty based on proof

The Podcast Reality Check

Milestone Percentile
1 episode Top 10%
21 episodes Top 1%

Alex spent 4.5 years making two podcasts a week — only getting 2-3K downloads/month. It took off at year 5 after he sold a company for $46M.

"The message is 10%. Context is 90%."

Elon tweets from the toilet → million likes. Why? Proof. Build your proof first.

Track Inputs, Not Outputs

Lagging (Output) Leading (Input)
Sales closed Calls made
Weight lost Calories tracked
Downloads Episodes posted

The Ultimate Win Definition

"The delta between how hard I tried and how hard I can possibly try. If that gap is zero, I have won."

Finite vs. Infinite Games

  • Finite: Known players, rules, clear winner → game ends
  • Infinite: Unknown players, no fixed rules → point is to keep playing

Marriage, health, business = infinite games. You don't "win" marriage. The point is to stay married.

Lesson 4: What I'd Tell My Younger, Poorer Self

Morning Routines Are a Trap

"People obsessed with morning routines make less money than people obsessed with making money."

A kid spent 3 hours daily on grounded walking, gratitude journal, ice plunge — barely had energy to work. Alex's advice: Replace 3 hours of routine with 3 hours of work.

Rich people did "whatever it took" → got rich → filled time with weird routines → told others the routines made them rich.

False Correlations

Wrong Logic Reality
Warren Buffett drinks Coke → Coke makes you rich Plenty of broke Coke drinkers exist
Hard childhood → success More unsuccessful people with hard childhoods
Tall people play basketball → Basketball makes you tall Correlation ≠ causation

Better approach: Look at what someone did on the way up, not what they do at the top.

"30 is the New 20" is a Lie

"It's an excuse to take 10 years longer to accomplish the same thing."

Mr. Beast started at 13. At 23, he has 10 years of reps. Someone starting at 45 with 5 years experience is behind a 23-year-old with 10 years. Pay down your ignorance debt as fast as possible.

Seasons of Learning vs. Earning

An 18-year-old editor wanted $100K/year starting salary. Alternative: only editor at a no-name company (no growth). Alex's offer: learn from a world-class media team → become creative director later.

The Right Hook

"Whenever you choose to earn rather than learn, you're right-hooking life and saying 'I'm no longer going to develop and this is good enough.'"

The Two Things He Didn't Understand

1. Product

Was obsessed with sales/marketing (fast feedback loops). $100 Million Offers: zero marketing, sells thousands of copies per day.

  • Old way: 2 months building product, 2 years marketing it
  • New way: 2 years building product, lifetime of zero marketing spend

2. Brand

Brand = what people learn to associate with you. Who you associate with becomes part of your brand.

The Effort Gap from Good to Great

Level Effort Required
8 → 9 10x work
9 → 9.5 10x more
9.5 → 9.75 10x more
"If you put 1,000 hours into something, it'll probably be pretty good. Most people spend less than 100."

Lesson 5: Making 2026 Your Best Year

The 4-Step Framework

  1. Eliminate distractions
  2. Get started
  3. Get better
  4. Never stop

What Your Spending Reveals

"Rich people buy time. Poor people buy stuff. Ambitious people buy skills. Lazy people buy distractions."

Your calendar + credit card = prediction of your future. You vote with your dollars about who you want to become.

Commitment = Elimination of Alternatives

Marriage = eliminating all other potential partners. Real commitment = removing everything that isn't the main thing.

Distraction #1: Bad Friends

5 Questions to Evaluate a Friendship:

  1. If someone said "you're a lot like [friend]," would that be a compliment?
  2. Are you truly fulfilled or just avoiding loneliness?
  3. Can you be unapologetically yourself around them?
  4. Do you like who they ARE today, or who they COULD be?
  5. Would you want your child to be friends with this person?
"Rare people are by definition rare. It's normal to have fewer of them."

Reference Group ≠ Who You Spend Time With

Harvard study misquoted: It's not the 5 people you spend time with, it's the 5 people you compare yourself to.

Even better: The people whose voices you hear when making big decisions. Listen to the people closest to your goals, not the people closest to you.

Distraction #2: Environment & Notifications

"If you can't sit still, ignore notifications, and focus on one task for 8 hours straight, never expect to build anything great."

Tactical Tips:

  • Phone to grayscale (reduces consumption ~30%)
  • Turn off ALL notifications
  • Use a timer to track REAL work time

The Jerry Seinfeld Method

Lock yourself in a room with only a notepad. You don't have to write, but you can't do anything else. Writing becomes the default.

"Focus occurs when you remove everything else. What's left is focus."

The 85-Year-Old Test

Ask any elderly person what they think about your "problems" → "Who cares?"

If that's what the wisest people think after having the most life experience, why not cheat code that into today?

Lesson 6: Brutal Truths That Give You an Unfair Advantage

1. Pain is the Price of Progress

"You cannot both want progress and live an easy life. These two things conflict."

2. Happy But Not Satisfied

Happy = content with your life (allowed). Satisfied = complacent, no more action (not allowed).

After a year of "retirement," Alex concluded: Hard work IS the goal. Results are just a secondary effect.

3. Ignore Critics

  • Average people want to keep you average
  • Some want you to fail to justify the risk THEY didn't take
  • Your critics will die → their opinion won't matter then → doesn't matter now

4. Selective Productivity (The Ladder Analogy)

The Wall

Wall = success threshold requiring critical mass. 4 short ladders (multiple projects) = none reach the top. Take all rungs → put on ONE ladder → get over the wall.

"Your plans aren't working because you're not working on the plan. The hard part isn't creating or following the plan — it's sticking with it."

5. Fear vs. Regret

"Your fear of regret must be greater than your fear of rejection."

Fear only exists in the vague. Break it into specifics. Actual downside = opinions of people you don't care about.

6. Persistence Creates Timing

"You can time everything perfectly if your intention is to never stop."

Don't try to hit 3 specific opportunity windows. Work the whole time → you'll always be ready.

7. Envy vs. Effort

Three hard truths:

  1. No one is doing as well as you think
  2. You win by growing, not beating people
  3. Your biggest threat = a mediocre version of YOU

8. Hard Conversations Create Opportunities

"Everything you want is on the other side of a few hard conversations you've been putting off."

Best trade: Short pain → Long gain. Worst trade: Short gain → Long pain (guaranteed loss, just delayed).

9. Endure

Signed gym lease with $5,000 in bank. Rent was $5,000. Had never made real money. Necessity is the mother of invention.

10. Results > Excuses

Leila's quote: "It's not your fault, but it is still your problem."

Excuses = permission slip for mediocrity.

11. The Hard Way IS the Easy Way

The easy way never gets you there. When a shortcut is found → everyone uses it → no longer a shortcut.

12. Don't Give Away Your Power

"What offends you controls you. Where you point blame, you point power."

13. Rejection vs. Regret

Losers fear rejection. Winners fear regret.

Champions are relieved when they win, not excited. They hate losing more than they love winning.

14. Consistency Beats Talent

"You can beat most people at anything if you stick with it for a year."

20 hours = basic competency at any skill. People wait 10 years to log the first 20 hours.

Lesson 7: Getting Rich in the Next 10 Years

Alex hit $100M net worth at 31, but lost all his money twice before that. These rules are the difference.

1. Passive Income is a Lie

Everyone trades time for money. It's a spectrum of "how active."

Calculate your hourly rate:

  • Last year's income ÷ 2,000 = hourly rate (40hr week)
  • Last year's income ÷ 8,000 = actual hourly rate (all waking hours)

If an activity pays more than that number, DO MORE OF IT.

2. If You Can't Buy It Twice, Don't Buy It Once

The Smoothie King Lesson

Making $6.75/hr after taxes. $13 lunch = 2 hours of work. A third of every shift just to eat.

"Would I work a whole shift if someone held up a shirt I didn't need?"

3. Overestimate a Year, Underestimate a Decade

"Would you sign a contract: make no money for 10 years, but add 1-2 zeros to your net worth at year 10?"

If yes → act like it.

Invest in S-M-E 500 (yourself), not S&P 500. You will always beat the market.

4. Only Get Rich Once

"Don't bet what you have and need for something you don't have and don't need."

Partnership rules (9 failed partnerships before Leila): A partner must have ONE thing you don't — Money, Experience, or Time/Energy — with clear contribution defined.

5. Manage Time Before Money

Money = encapsulated time. For some, $100 = 4 hours. For others, $100 = 4 minutes.

6. Check Bank Account Before Social Media

"As I started paying attention to my money, my money started paying attention to me."

Peter Drucker: "What gets measured gets managed."

7. Learn to Make Money Before Making Money Make Money

Don't put $1,000 into Dogecoin. Put it into skills.

"50% improvement in your skill set → 50% increase in annual income. That beats any stock return."

8. The 5-9 Rule

Everyone has 5am-9am and 5pm-9pm. That's 8 hours/day even with a full-time job.

Work makes money two ways: you earn it, and you're not spending it.

9. If You Don't Know How You Can Lose, You Will Lose

"If something looks amazing and you think 'there's no way I can lose,' you're absolutely going to lose your ass."

Multiply your estimated risk by 3.

10. Ignorance is Your Biggest Cost

Goal Current Cost of Ignorance
$1M/year $50K/year $950K/year

11. Two Types of Knowledge

Type What It Is Example
Declarative Knowing something exists "Facebook ads exist"
Procedural Knowing how to do it "Here's how to run them"

Document → Demonstrate → Duplicate (how Alex learned Facebook ads in 8 sessions at $750/hr)

12. A Rolex Won't Replace Reality

Status symbols don't give you status. Work does.

Focus = The Number of Things You Say NO To

12 hours/day for 30 days straight, no weekends. What you learn:

  1. You aren't made of glass
  2. How much more you COULD be working
  3. How much faster you could achieve goals
  4. People working more than you ARE beating you

Work Begins When Motivation Stops

"Your working reps begin once you've achieved failure." (Old bodybuilding principle)

Muhammad Ali: "I hated every minute of training, but I told myself: suffer now and live the rest of your life as a champion."

Learning to Work is a Skill

Legal partner to young Alex: "That's cute. You don't even know what work is. You spend time here, but you don't know how to work."

They Cannot Copy Your Proof

People can copy: promises, services, logos, names. People CANNOT copy: what you've done.

Mr. Beast example: First viral video = counting to a million. Not teaching. DOING something no one else would.

Model the Rise, Not the Plateau

"You don't get rich by flying on private jets. You get rich, THEN you fly on private jets."

Act With Urgency

Instead of... Ask... Multiplier

Tomorrow Isn't Guaranteed

  • 3 in 10,000 people live to 100

  • At 37, you're at the halfway point

Lesson 9: For Ambitious People in Their 20s & 30s

1. Take Asymmetric Bets

Under 30 with nothing? Your downside is zero. Your upside is everything.

"You never actually start from scratch ever again. After the first time, you start with experience."

Alex had 9 failed businesses before one worked. 6 of those were at the same time.

2. Don't Follow Your Passion

"Passion comes from competence, not creates competence."

Your passions WILL change as you age. Getting good at anything requires boring, monotonous repetition.

Better approach: Find things you're good at that people already spend money on.

3. Get Good at ONE Thing

"Do you remember that guy who got rich dropshipping, day trading, buying crypto, and wholesaling all at the same time? Me neither."

Leila's intervention: "You're clearly good at this one thing. Why don't we cut everything else?"

"If you want to impress poor people, talk about how many things you do. If you want to impress rich people, talk about how good you are at ONE thing."

4. Stop Networking (For Now)

Two seasons: Exploration (broad, tasting) and Exploitation (deep, focused).

"If you're choosing between a networking lunch and working — choose the work. When the work works out, the people will still be there."

Winners isolate. Losers congregate. The only way into circles above you = work ethic.

5. Money Loves Speed

"By the time you have all the information, the opportunity is gone."

Decision math: 5 min to decide + 10 min to fix if wrong = 15 min total. 2 hours deliberating = 2 hours wasted even if right.

"The heaviest things in life aren't iron or gold — they're unmade decisions."

6. Pay Off Ignorance Debt

"An expert is just somebody who's made all the mistakes you can make in a very narrow field."

7. Name the Voice

Alex almost didn't sell Gym Launch for $46M because he thought a colleague wouldn't be impressed.

"Whose voice is in your head? Is that the person you want to give control over your entire life?"

8. Get Over Yourself

"Everyone's childhood was difficult. Get over it."
Losers Winners
Define themselves by what happened TO them Define themselves by what they make happen DESPITE what happened
See disadvantages as excuses See disadvantages as origin stories

9. Solve Bigger Problems

"If you want to make a million dollars, you have to endure a million dollars worth of pain."

Steven Schwarzman insight: Big goals and small goals are usually just as hard. If suffering is constant anyway, might as well go big.

10. Obsession is the Ticket of Entry

"Normal people call what I consider sane people to be obsessed. Sane people consider everyone else insane because they do nothing and waste their lives."

11. Work Hard AND Smart

"Work smart not hard only works when competing against people who aren't smart."

Take 40 years of career work → cram into 4 years → have 36 years of freedom with resources.

12. Accept Boredom

The Marathon Visual

People cheer at the start line. People cheer at the finish line. Where does winning happen? In the middle. Alone. Uncheered.

"Consistency never looks impressive in the moment — only at the end."

13. Accept Sacrifice

"There's no perfect way to live your 20s or 30s. You either live them up and become an underskilled 40-year-old, or work them up and become an underlived 40-year-old."

Every hour spent NOT pursuing your goal = declaring that thing more important than your goal.

The 60-Second Summary

If everything was deleted and Alex had 60 seconds, this is what he'd say:

"Figure out what you want.
Ignore the opinions of others.
And do so much volume that it would be
unreasonable to not be successful."

This isn't motivation. This is math. The person who does 10x the volume, gets 10x the reps, learns 10x the lessons, and compounds 10x the experience will — by sheer mathematics — end up further than everyone who didn't.

The glass at the beginning of the rope will cut you. The loneliness of outworking your friend group will hurt. The boredom of doing the same thing for years without visible results will test you.

But on the other side?

Everything you ever wanted — built by the only person who could have built it: you.

Now go pull the rope.
